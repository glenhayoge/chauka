# What is M&E?

Monitoring and Evaluation (M&E) is a systematic process used by development organisations to track progress, measure results, and learn from their work. Chauka is built to support this process.

## Monitoring vs Evaluation

These two terms are often used together, but they serve different purposes:

### Monitoring

Monitoring is the **ongoing, routine** collection of data to track whether a project is on course.

- Happens **continuously** throughout the project
- Answers: "Are we doing what we planned? Are we on track?"
- Focuses on **inputs, activities, and outputs**
- Uses **quantitative data** (numbers, percentages, counts)
- Involves **regular reporting** (monthly, quarterly)

In Chauka, monitoring happens primarily in the **Monitor** tab, where you enter data against indicator targets.

### Evaluation

Evaluation is a **periodic, in-depth** assessment of whether a project is achieving its intended results and why.

- Happens at **specific points** (mid-term, end of project)
- Answers: "Are we making a difference? Why or why not?"
- Focuses on **outcomes and impact**
- Uses both **quantitative and qualitative data**
- Involves **analysis, judgement, and recommendations**

Chauka supports evaluation by providing the data foundation — well-structured indicators, target-actual comparisons, and historical trends.

## Why M&E matters

M&E serves several critical functions:

### Accountability
- Demonstrating to donors, partners, and communities that resources are being used effectively
- Providing evidence that commitments are being met

### Learning
- Understanding what works and what doesn't
- Adapting strategies based on evidence
- Building institutional knowledge

### Decision-making
- Identifying problems early so they can be addressed
- Allocating resources where they're needed most
- Justifying programme continuation, expansion, or closure

### Communication
- Telling the story of your work with evidence
- Sharing results with stakeholders
- Contributing to sector-wide learning

## The M&E cycle

M&E is not a one-time activity. It follows a cycle:

1. **Plan** — define what you'll measure, how, and when
2. **Collect** — gather data through surveys, observation, records
3. **Analyse** — compare actuals against targets, identify trends
4. **Report** — share findings with stakeholders
5. **Learn** — draw lessons and adapt your approach
6. **Repeat** — continue the cycle throughout the project

Chauka supports every stage: you **plan** in the Design tab, **collect** in the Monitor tab, **analyse** on the Dashboard, **report** through exports, and **learn** from trends over time.

## M&E jargon explained

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Baseline** | Starting point measurement before the project begins |
| **Target** | The planned value you aim to achieve |
| **Actual** | The real value achieved, based on collected data |
| **Disaggregation** | Breaking down data by category (gender, age, location) |
| **Logframe** | A matrix summarising results, indicators, and assumptions |
| **Theory of Change** | A model explaining how your activities lead to impact |
| **SMART indicators** | Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound |
