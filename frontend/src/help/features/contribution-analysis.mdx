# Contribution Analysis

The Contribution tab shows how output-level progress contributes to outcome-level results. It uses weighted contribution factors and actual indicator data to calculate an overall score for each outcome.

## What is contribution analysis?

In a results hierarchy, outputs are expected to lead to outcomes. But not all outputs contribute equally — some are more critical than others. Contribution analysis lets you:

- Assign **weights** to outputs reflecting their relative importance
- Calculate a **weighted progress score** for each outcome based on its outputs' indicator data
- Visualise which outputs are driving progress and which are lagging

## Enabling contribution analysis

Contribution analysis must be enabled in your logframe settings:

1. Open your logframe
2. Go to the **Settings** tab
3. Find **Contribution analysis** and toggle it on
4. Save

Once enabled, the **Contribution** tab becomes active.

## Setting contribution weights

Each output-level result has a **contribution weight** that reflects how much it contributes to its parent outcome. By default, all results have a weight of 100 (equal contribution).

To adjust weights:

1. Go to the **Design** tab
2. Find the output-level result you want to adjust
3. Edit the **contribution weight** field
4. Repeat for sibling outputs under the same outcome

Weights are relative — what matters is the proportion, not the absolute number. For example, weights of 60, 30, and 10 mean the first output contributes 60% to the outcome score.

## How scores are calculated

Chauka calculates contribution scores by:

1. For each output, finding its indicators and their most recent actual vs target values
2. Calculating indicator progress as a percentage (actual / target x 100)
3. Weighting each output's progress by its contribution weight
4. Summing the weighted progress to produce the outcome score

For example:

| Output | Progress | Weight | Weighted contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Output 1.1: Farmers trained | 80% | 50% | 40% |
| Output 1.2: Seeds distributed | 60% | 30% | 18% |
| Output 1.3: Extension visits | 40% | 20% | 8% |
| **Outcome 1 score** | | | **66%** |

## Reading the contribution tree

The Contribution tab displays a tree view:

- **Outcome cards** show the parent result with its overall contribution score
- A **progress bar** shows the score visually
- **Child outputs** are listed below with their individual progress, weight, and mini progress bar
- Scores are **colour-coded**:
  - **Green** (70% and above) — on track
  - **Amber** (40-69%) — needs attention
  - **Red** (below 40%) — off track

## Prerequisites

For contribution analysis to produce meaningful results, you need:

- Contribution analysis enabled in Settings
- Results structured as a hierarchy (outcomes with child outputs)
- Indicators attached to output-level results
- Targets set for those indicators
- Actual data entered in the Monitor tab

If any of these are missing, the contribution score may show as zero or "No data available".

## Use cases

- **Programme reviews** — show stakeholders a quantified view of which outputs are driving change
- **Donor reporting** — demonstrate progress with weighted evidence
- **Adaptive management** — identify underperforming outputs that need more resources or a revised approach
- **Strategic planning** — understand which interventions have the greatest influence on outcomes

## Tips

- **Start with equal weights** — adjust only when you have evidence that some outputs matter more
- **Review weights periodically** — as your understanding of the programme evolves, weights may need updating
- **Ensure data is current** — the score is only as good as the latest data entered in Monitor
- **Use alongside disaggregation** — contribution analysis tells you how much progress; disaggregation tells you who is benefiting
