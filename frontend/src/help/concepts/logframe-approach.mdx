# The Logframe Approach

The logical framework (logframe) is one of the most widely used tools in international development for planning, monitoring, and evaluating projects. Chauka is built around this approach.

## What is a logframe?

A logframe is a structured matrix that summarises the key elements of a project:

- **What** you aim to achieve (results)
- **How** you'll measure success (indicators)
- **What** must be true for success (assumptions)
- **What** you'll do (activities)

It creates a clear logical chain from activities to long-term impact.

## The logframe matrix

The traditional logframe is a 4x4 matrix:

| | Results | Indicators | Means of Verification | Assumptions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | Long-term goal | How you'll measure it | Where the data comes from | External conditions |
| **Outcome** | Medium-term change | How you'll measure it | Where the data comes from | External conditions |
| **Output** | Direct deliverable | How you'll measure it | Where the data comes from | External conditions |
| **Activity** | What you'll do | Resources needed | | Preconditions |

Chauka implements this matrix as an interactive tool where each element is linked and trackable.

## The vertical logic

The logframe reads from bottom to top:

> **IF** we complete these **activities**, **AND** assumptions hold, **THEN** we produce these **outputs**.
>
> **IF** we produce these **outputs**, **AND** assumptions hold, **THEN** we achieve these **outcomes**.
>
> **IF** we achieve these **outcomes**, **AND** assumptions hold, **THEN** we contribute to this **impact**.

This "if-then" chain is the vertical logic. It tells the story of how your work leads to change.

### Example

> **IF** we train 200 community health workers (activity) and the government continues to employ them (assumption), **THEN** health services are delivered in 50 communities (output).
>
> **IF** health services are delivered (output) and communities use them (assumption), **THEN** maternal and child health improves (outcome).
>
> **IF** health improves (outcome) and other health determinants remain stable (assumption), **THEN** child mortality reduces (impact).

## The horizontal logic

The logframe also reads left to right:

- **Result** — what you want to achieve
- **Indicator** — how you'll measure it (specific, measurable)
- **Means of verification** — where you'll get the data
- **Assumptions** — what must be true externally

This horizontal logic ensures that every result is measurable and that you've thought about external risks.

## How Chauka implements the logframe

| Logframe element | Chauka feature |
|---|---|
| Results hierarchy | Results tree in Design tab |
| Indicators | Indicators with sub-indicators |
| Targets | Targets set per reporting period |
| Means of verification | Indicator descriptions and data sources |
| Assumptions | Assumptions with risk ratings |
| Activities | Activities with milestones |
| Resources | Budget lines and resource assignments |

## Strengths of the logframe approach

- **Clarity** — forces you to think through the logic of your intervention
- **Measurability** — every result has indicators and targets
- **Accountability** — clear framework for reporting to donors
- **Communication** — shared language across teams and stakeholders
- **Risk awareness** — explicit identification of assumptions

## Limitations to be aware of

- **Rigidity** — logframes can become too fixed if not updated regularly
- **Linearity** — real-world change is often non-linear and complex
- **Over-simplification** — complex programmes may not fit neatly into a matrix
- **Indicator bias** — risk of measuring what's easy rather than what matters

Chauka helps address some of these limitations by making logframes living documents that are easy to update, with dashboards that highlight trends rather than just numbers.

## Logframes and theories of change

Many organisations now use a **Theory of Change** (ToC) alongside the logframe. The ToC provides a richer narrative about how change happens, while the logframe provides the measurement framework. Chauka's results tree can capture both the hierarchy (logframe) and the causal logic (theory of change).

See [Theory of Change](/help/concepts/theory-of-change) for more on this relationship.
