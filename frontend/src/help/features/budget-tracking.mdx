# Budget Tracking

Chauka lets you track budgets and expenses against your activities. The Budget tab gives you visibility into how funds are being used across your logframe.

## How budgets work in Chauka

Budgets are linked to activities through **budget lines**. Each activity can have one or more budget lines, and you record expenses against those lines.

```
Activity: Conduct geological surveys
  Budget Line: Consultant fees — $15,000
  Budget Line: Travel & transport — $5,000
  Budget Line: Equipment rental — $3,000
```

## Creating budget lines

1. Open the **Budget** tab (or navigate to an activity in the Design tab)
2. Find the activity you want to budget
3. Click **Add Budget Line**
4. Enter:
   - **Description** — what the cost covers (e.g., "Consultant fees")
   - **Budgeted amount** — how much is allocated
5. Save

## Recording expenses

As money is spent, record expenses against budget lines:

1. In the **Budget** tab, find the relevant budget line
2. Enter the **actual expense** for the current reporting period
3. Save

Chauka tracks the running total so you can see:
- **Budgeted** — how much was planned
- **Spent** — how much has been used
- **Remaining** — how much is left
- **Variance** — over or under budget

## The Budget tab view

The Budget tab shows a grid view:

- **Rows** are activities and their budget lines
- **Columns** are reporting periods
- Each cell shows the expense for that period

At the bottom, you'll see totals:
- Total budget for the logframe
- Total spent to date
- Remaining balance

## Budget vs actual analysis

Chauka automatically calculates variances:

| Budget Line | Budgeted | Spent | Remaining | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consultant fees | $15,000 | $12,000 | $3,000 | On track |
| Travel | $5,000 | $6,200 | -$1,200 | Over budget |
| Equipment | $3,000 | $1,500 | $1,500 | Under budget |

This helps you spot potential issues early:
- Lines that are overspending need investigation
- Lines with lots of remaining budget might indicate delayed activities

## Best practices

- **Budget at the activity level** — this links spending directly to deliverables
- **Use descriptive budget line names** — future you will thank present you
- **Record expenses regularly** — don't wait until the end of the quarter
- **Review variances monthly** — catch overspending before it becomes a problem
- **Document significant variances** — explain why spending is higher or lower than planned
