# Creating Your First Logframe

This guide walks you through creating a logframe from scratch and populating it with your first results, indicators, and activities.

## Prerequisites

Before creating a logframe, you need:

- An organisation set up in Chauka
- At least one programme and one project (logframes belong to projects)
- Lead or Admin role for the relevant project

If you haven't set these up yet, see [Organisations, Programs & Projects](/help/features/organisations-programs-projects).

## Step 1: Create the logframe

1. Navigate to your project
2. Click **Create Logframe** (or the equivalent button in your project view)
3. Give the logframe a **name** — typically the project name or a descriptive title (e.g., "Water & Sanitation Programme 2024-2027")
4. The logframe will be created and you'll be taken to its **Design** tab

## Step 2: Configure reporting periods

Before adding results and indicators, set up your reporting periods:

1. Go to the **Settings** tab of your logframe
2. Define your **reporting periods** (e.g., quarterly: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 for each year)
3. These periods will be used when you set targets and enter monitoring data

## Step 3: Build the results tree

In the **Design** tab, start building your results hierarchy:

1. **Add your Impact** (the top-level goal) — e.g., "Improved health outcomes in target communities"
2. Under the Impact, **add Outcomes** — e.g., "Increased access to clean water"
3. Under each Outcome, **add Outputs** — e.g., "50 boreholes constructed in target villages"

Each level represents a step in your theory of change, from activities at the bottom to long-term impact at the top.

> **Tip:** Start with 1 Impact, 2-3 Outcomes, and 3-5 Outputs per Outcome. You can always add more later.

## Step 4: Add indicators

For each result, add indicators that will measure progress:

1. Click on a result to expand it
2. Click **Add Indicator**
3. Define the indicator:
   - **Name** — what you're measuring (e.g., "Number of boreholes constructed")
   - **Sub-indicators** — breakdown categories if needed (e.g., by region, by type)
4. Set **targets** for each reporting period

## Step 5: Add assumptions

Assumptions capture the conditions that need to hold true for your results to be achieved:

1. Under each result, click **Add Assumption**
2. Describe the assumption (e.g., "Government continues to approve borehole permits")
3. Assign a **risk rating** to indicate how likely the assumption is to fail

## Step 6: Plan activities

Switch to the **Workload** tab or add activities within the Design tab:

1. Under an Output, click **Add Activity**
2. Describe the activity (e.g., "Conduct geological surveys at 50 sites")
3. Add **milestones** with target dates
4. Optionally add **budget lines** to track costs

## Step 7: Start monitoring

Once your logframe is designed, move to the **Monitor** tab:

1. Select the current reporting period
2. Enter actual values against your indicator targets
3. Add status updates to activities
4. Review the **Dashboard** for an overview of progress

## What a complete logframe looks like

```
Impact: Improved health outcomes in target communities
  Outcome 1: Increased access to clean water
    Output 1.1: 50 boreholes constructed
      Indicator: Number of boreholes completed (target: 50)
      Activity: Conduct geological surveys
      Activity: Procure drilling equipment
    Output 1.2: Community water committees established
      Indicator: Number of committees trained (target: 50)
      Activity: Recruit committee members
      Activity: Deliver training workshops
  Outcome 2: Improved hygiene practices
    Output 2.1: Hygiene awareness campaigns delivered
      Indicator: Number of people reached (target: 10,000)
      Activity: Develop campaign materials
      Activity: Conduct community sessions
```
