# Reporting & Learning

Monitoring data is only valuable when it's used. This guide covers how to turn your Chauka data into meaningful reports and actionable learning.

## The purpose of reporting

Reporting serves different audiences with different needs:

| Audience | What they need | How often |
|---|---|---|
| **Donors** | Progress against targets, budget utilisation, narrative on challenges | Quarterly or as per grant agreement |
| **Senior management** | Portfolio overview, strategic issues, resource allocation decisions | Monthly or quarterly |
| **Programme team** | Detailed progress, what needs attention, upcoming milestones | Weekly or fortnightly |
| **Communities** | What's been achieved, what's coming next, how they can participate | Periodic community meetings |
| **Partners** | Shared results, coordination needs, joint learning | As per partnership agreements |

## Types of reports

### Progress reports
Regular reports showing progress against your logframe:
- Indicator status (target vs actual for the reporting period)
- Activity completion and milestone tracking
- Budget utilisation
- Challenges and mitigation measures
- Plans for the next period

Chauka supports this through the **Dashboard**, **Overview**, and **Excel export**.

### Annual reviews
Comprehensive assessments at the end of each year:
- Full-year indicator performance
- Analysis of trends and patterns
- Lessons learned
- Recommendations for the coming year

Use Chauka's historical data across all reporting periods for this.

### Donor reports
Formatted to meet specific donor requirements:
1. Export data from Chauka
2. Transfer into the donor's template
3. Add narrative sections and analysis
4. Include supporting evidence

### Internal learning reports
Focused on what worked, what didn't, and why:
- Analyse variance between targets and actuals
- Examine assumptions — which held, which didn't?
- Document adaptations made and their results
- Capture unexpected outcomes (positive and negative)

## Getting data out of Chauka

### Dashboard review
The fastest way to assess overall progress. Use the Dashboard for:
- Quick team stand-ups
- Management briefings
- Identifying indicators that need attention

### Overview tab
For more detailed review meetings. The Overview shows:
- Full results framework with status
- All indicators with current values
- Assumptions and risk ratings

### Excel export
For detailed analysis and donor reporting:
- Export the full logframe dataset
- Manipulate data in Excel for custom analyses
- Create charts and visualisations
- Format for donor templates

### Print view
For offline sharing:
- Clean, formatted view of the logframe
- Suitable for meeting handouts
- No login required for viewing

## From data to learning

Collecting and reporting data is not the end goal — **learning** is. Here's how to turn your Chauka data into organisational learning:

### 1. Analyse patterns, not just numbers
Don't just report that "85% of target was achieved." Ask:
- Why 85% and not 100%?
- Which areas performed better? Why?
- What was different this period compared to last?

### 2. Examine assumptions
Review the assumptions in your logframe:
- Which assumptions are holding true?
- Which are at risk? What does that mean for your results?
- Do any assumptions need to be updated?

### 3. Look at disaggregated data
Sub-indicator data often reveals important patterns:
- Are certain groups being reached less than others?
- Are there geographic disparities?
- What does this mean for equity and inclusion?

### 4. Connect outputs to outcomes
The most important learning question:
- Are your outputs leading to the expected outcomes?
- If outputs are delivered but outcomes aren't changing, what's the gap?
- Does your theory of change need updating?

### 5. Document and share
Learning only has value if it's captured and shared:
- Record lessons in team meetings
- Share insights with partners
- Update your theory of change based on evidence
- Apply lessons to future programme design

## Using Chauka data for adaptive management

Adaptive management means adjusting your approach based on evidence. Chauka supports this by:

1. **Showing real-time progress** — the Dashboard highlights what's on track and what isn't
2. **Tracking trends** — historical data across periods shows whether things are improving
3. **Flagging risks** — assumption risk ratings warn you about external threats
4. **Enabling comparison** — disaggregated data reveals where to focus effort

When data shows something isn't working:
1. Investigate the root cause (not just the symptoms)
2. Discuss with your team and stakeholders
3. Decide whether to adjust activities, reallocate resources, or update targets
4. Document the decision and rationale
5. Monitor whether the adjustment works

## Best practices for reporting and learning

- **Report on time** — late reports lose their value for decision-making
- **Be honest about challenges** — donors and managers prefer transparency to spin
- **Use visuals** — charts and graphs communicate trends better than tables of numbers
- **Include "so what?"** — every data point should come with analysis and implications
- **Create a learning rhythm** — regular reflection meetings, not just end-of-project evaluations
- **Close the loop** — show how previous lessons have been applied
