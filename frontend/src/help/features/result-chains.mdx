# Building Result Chains

The results tree is the backbone of your logframe. It captures your project's theory of change as a hierarchy of expected results, from the activities you do to the long-term impact you aim to achieve.

## The results hierarchy

Chauka supports a multi-level results hierarchy. The default levels are:

| Level | What it represents | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact** | Long-term goal or vision | "Reduced child mortality in Northern Province" |
| **Outcome** | Medium-term change that contributes to the impact | "Improved maternal and child healthcare access" |
| **Output** | Direct deliverable produced by your activities | "200 community health workers trained" |

Results are nested: Outputs sit under Outcomes, which sit under Impacts. This nesting creates a chain that tells the story of how your work leads to change.

## Creating results

### Adding an Impact

1. Go to the **Design** tab of your logframe
2. Click **Add Result** at the top level
3. Enter the impact statement — describe the long-term change you're contributing to
4. Save

Most logframes have one Impact, though Chauka supports multiple.

### Adding Outcomes

1. Click on your Impact to expand it
2. Click **Add Child Result** (or the add button below the Impact)
3. Enter the outcome statement — describe the medium-term change
4. Save

Typically, a logframe has 2-4 Outcomes.

### Adding Outputs

1. Click on an Outcome to expand it
2. Click **Add Child Result**
3. Enter the output statement — describe what your project will deliver
4. Save

Each Outcome typically has 2-5 Outputs.

## Editing results

- Click on any result's text to edit it inline
- Use drag-and-drop (if available) to reorder results within the same level
- Results cascade: deleting a parent will remove all its children, indicators, and activities

> **Warning:** Deleting a result removes all nested content. Make sure you want to remove the entire branch before confirming.

## Structuring effective results

### Write clear result statements

Good result statements describe a **change**, not an activity:

- **Good**: "Increased school enrolment among girls aged 6-12"
- **Poor**: "Build schools" (this is an activity, not a result)

### Keep the hierarchy logical

Each level should answer "how?" for the level above:

- **Impact**: Why does this matter? (long-term goal)
- **Outcome**: What change will achieve it? (medium-term)
- **Output**: What will we deliver? (short-term, tangible)

### Use numbering conventions

Chauka displays results in order. A common convention:

```
1. Impact: Improved livelihoods
  1.1 Outcome: Increased agricultural productivity
    1.1.1 Output: Farmers trained in modern techniques
    1.1.2 Output: Irrigation systems installed
  1.2 Outcome: Improved market access
    1.2.1 Output: Road network rehabilitated
    1.2.2 Output: Market information system deployed
```

## Custom result levels

In logframe **Settings**, you can customise the number of levels and their labels. This is useful if your organisation uses different terminology:

- "Goal" instead of "Impact"
- "Purpose" instead of "Outcome"
- "Result" instead of "Output"
- Additional sub-levels if your framework requires them

## Assumptions on results

Each result can have **assumptions** — external conditions that must hold true for the result to be achieved. See [Indicators & Targets](/help/features/indicators-targets) for how to attach indicators, and the Design tab for adding assumptions with risk ratings.
