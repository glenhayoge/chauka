# Indicators & Targets

Indicators are how you measure whether your results are being achieved. Each indicator has targets (what you plan to achieve) and actuals (what actually happened), tracked across reporting periods.

## What is an indicator?

An indicator is a specific, measurable metric attached to a result. For example:

| Result | Indicator |
|---|---|
| "50 boreholes constructed" | Number of boreholes completed |
| "Improved school attendance" | Percentage of enrolled students attending regularly |
| "Community committees established" | Number of functioning committees |

Good indicators are **SMART**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

## Creating indicators

You can create indicators from scratch or use a pre-built indicator from the library.

### From scratch

1. In the **Design** tab, click on a result to expand it
2. Click **Add Indicator**
3. Fill in the indicator details:
   - **Name** — a clear description of what you're measuring
   - **Description** — additional context, measurement methodology, or data source
4. Save the indicator

### From the indicator library

Chauka includes an indicator library with reusable, pre-built indicators that can be shared across logframes.

1. In the **Design** tab, click on a result to expand it
2. Click **Use from Library**
3. Browse or search indicators by keyword, sector, result level, or framework
4. Click on an indicator to preview its details (definition, unit of measure, data source, collection method, and more)
5. Click **Use this indicator** to add it to your result

The indicator is copied into your logframe with all its metadata pre-filled, including a default sub-indicator. You can edit it after adding.

For more about the indicator library, see [Indicator Library](/help/features/indicator-library).

## Sub-indicators

Sub-indicators let you break down an indicator into categories. This is useful when you need **disaggregated data**.

For example, the indicator "Number of people trained" might have sub-indicators:
- Male
- Female
- Youth (under 25)

To add sub-indicators:

1. Click on an indicator to expand it
2. Click **Add Sub-indicator**
3. Name the sub-indicator (e.g., "Male", "Female")
4. Repeat for each disaggregation category

When you enter data, you'll enter values for each sub-indicator separately.

## Setting targets

Targets define what you plan to achieve in each reporting period.

1. Go to the **Monitor** tab
2. Find your indicator
3. For each reporting period (column), enter the **target value**
4. If you have sub-indicators, set targets for each one

Targets are two-dimensional: they combine an indicator (or sub-indicator) with a reporting period. This lets you plan a trajectory, for example:

| Period | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Target | 10 | 15 | 15 | 10 | 50 |

## Entering actuals

As you collect monitoring data, enter the actual values:

1. In the **Monitor** tab, find the indicator and reporting period
2. Enter the **actual value** in the data entry field
3. Save

Chauka will show you target vs actual comparisons so you can see whether you're on track.

## Tags

You can tag indicators to group and filter them. Tags are useful for:

- Cross-cutting themes (e.g., "Gender", "Climate")
- Donor reporting categories
- Custom groupings for your team

To add tags, edit the indicator and select or create tags from the available options.

## Indicator status and ratings

Indicators can have status ratings that give a quick visual assessment:

- **On track** — actuals are meeting or exceeding targets
- **At risk** — actuals are falling behind targets
- **Off track** — significant shortfall against targets

These ratings help managers quickly identify which indicators need attention during reviews.

## Disaggregation categories

Sub-indicators can be linked to **disaggregation categories** for structured analysis. For example, you might create a "Gender" category and tag sub-indicators "Male" and "Female" with it.

Once tagged, you can view automatic breakdown analysis in the **Disaggregation** tab. See [Disaggregation Analysis](/help/features/disaggregation-analysis) for details.

To set up disaggregation categories, go to **Settings > Disaggregation** in your logframe.

## Best practices

- **One indicator per measurable outcome** — don't try to capture everything in one indicator
- **Use sub-indicators for disaggregation** — rather than creating separate indicators for male/female, use sub-indicators
- **Tag sub-indicators with disaggregation categories** — this enables automatic breakdown analysis
- **Use the indicator library** — save time by starting with pre-built indicators and adapting them
- **Set realistic targets** — base targets on baseline data and programme capacity
- **Document your data sources** — in the indicator description, note where the data comes from and how it's collected
- **Review targets periodically** — adjust if circumstances change (document why)
