# Understanding the Logframe

The logframe (logical framework) is the heart of Chauka. It's a structured view of what your project aims to achieve, how you'll measure success, and what assumptions must hold true.

## What's inside a logframe

A logframe in Chauka contains:

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Results tree** | Hierarchical chain of Impact, Outcomes, and Outputs |
| **Indicators** | Measurable metrics attached to results, with targets and actuals |
| **Assumptions** | Conditions that must be true for your results to be achieved |
| **Activities** | The work your team does to deliver outputs |
| **Budget** | Financial tracking against activities |
| **Reporting periods** | Time divisions for setting targets and entering data |

## The logframe tabs

When you open a logframe, you see several tabs across the top:

### Dashboard
A summary view showing:
- Overall progress against targets
- Key metrics and statistics
- Recent activity and updates

### Overview
A high-level view of the entire results framework. Useful for presentations, reviews, and getting the big picture.

### Design
Where you build and edit your logframe structure:
- Add and rearrange results
- Create indicators with sub-indicators
- Define assumptions and risk ratings
- Structure your theory of change

### Monitor
Where day-to-day data entry happens:
- Enter actual values against indicator targets
- View progress by reporting period
- Compare targets vs actuals across sub-indicators

### Budget
Financial tracking:
- Budget lines linked to activities
- Expense recording
- Budget vs actual comparisons

### Workload
Activity management:
- View all activities and their status
- Track milestones and timelines
- Update progress on deliverables

### People
Team management for this logframe:
- See who has access
- View roles and permissions

### Settings
Configuration options:
- Define result levels and their labels
- Set up reporting periods (columns)
- Configure logframe preferences

## How data flows through the logframe

1. **Design** your results and indicators (Design tab)
2. **Set targets** for each indicator across reporting periods (Monitor tab)
3. **Collect data** and enter actuals (Monitor tab)
4. **Track activities** and update milestones (Workload tab)
5. **Record expenses** against budget lines (Budget tab)
6. **Review progress** on the Dashboard and Overview

## Logframe configuration

In the Settings tab, you can customise:

### Result levels
By default, Chauka uses three levels: Impact, Outcome, Output. You can rename these to match your organisation's terminology (e.g., Goal, Purpose, Result).

### Reporting periods
Define the time periods you'll use for targets and data entry. Common patterns include:
- **Quarterly**: Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 per year
- **Semi-annual**: H1, H2 per year
- **Annual**: One period per year
- **Monthly**: 12 periods per year

### Open result level
Controls which level of the results tree is expanded by default when viewing the logframe.
