# Joining an Organisation

Organisations are the top-level unit in Chauka. Every programme, project, and logframe belongs to an organisation. You need to either join an existing one or create a new one before you can start working.

## Joining an existing organisation

The most common path is to be invited by your organisation's administrator:

1. Ask your admin to send you an invitation from the **People** section of organisation settings
2. You'll receive an email with an invitation link
3. Click the link — if you're already signed in, you'll be added immediately
4. If you don't have an account yet, you'll create one during the process

Once you've joined, you'll see the organisation listed on your organisation selection screen.

## Your role in the organisation

When you join, your administrator assigns you a role that determines what you can do:

| Role | What you can do |
|---|---|
| **Organisation Admin** | Full access: manage members, create programmes/projects, edit all logframes |
| **Project Lead** | Edit logframes assigned to your projects, manage team within those projects |
| **Data Collector** | Enter data against indicators in assigned logframes |
| **Viewer** | View logframes and dashboards (read-only) |

Your role may differ across projects. For example, you might be a Lead on one project and a Viewer on another.

## Switching organisations

If you belong to multiple organisations:

1. Click your profile or the organisation name in the navigation
2. Select **Switch organisation** or go to the organisation selection screen
3. Choose the organisation you want to work in

## Organisation settings

Organisation admins can manage settings including:

- **Organisation details** — name, description
- **Members** — invite new members, change roles, remove members
- **Programmes & Projects** — create and manage the organisational hierarchy
