# Activities & Work Plans

Activities are the concrete actions your team takes to deliver outputs. In Chauka, activities are linked to results and can include milestones, timelines, status updates, and resource assignments.

## What is an activity?

An activity describes a specific piece of work. It sits under a result (typically an Output) in your logframe:

```
Output: 50 boreholes constructed
  Activity: Conduct geological surveys
  Activity: Procure drilling equipment
  Activity: Drill and install boreholes
  Activity: Train community water committees
```

## Creating activities

1. In the **Design** tab, click on a result (usually an Output) to expand it
2. Click **Add Activity**
3. Enter the activity details:
   - **Name** — a clear description of the work (e.g., "Conduct baseline survey")
   - **Description** — additional context or methodology
4. Save

## Milestones

Milestones are key checkpoints within an activity. They help you track whether an activity is progressing on schedule.

To add milestones:

1. Expand an activity
2. Click **Add Milestone**
3. Enter the milestone description and target date
4. Save

Examples of milestones:
- "Survey instrument finalised" — 15 March
- "Data collection complete" — 30 April
- "Final report submitted" — 31 May

## Status updates

Keep your team informed by adding status updates to activities:

1. Click on an activity
2. Click **Add Status Update**
3. Describe the current status, any issues, and next steps
4. Save

Status updates create a timeline of progress that's visible to the whole team.

## The Workload tab

The **Workload** tab gives you a unified view of all activities across your logframe:

- See all activities grouped by result
- View milestone timelines
- Check which activities are on track, delayed, or completed
- Filter and sort to focus on what matters

This is especially useful for project managers who need to coordinate multiple work streams.

## Resources

You can assign resources to activities to track who is responsible:

1. Open an activity
2. Add **Resources** — these can be team members, equipment, or other inputs
3. This helps with workload planning and accountability

## Timeline lines (TA Lines)

TA Lines represent the time allocation for activities across reporting periods. They help you plan when activities will take place:

1. Open an activity
2. Set the TA Line to indicate which periods the activity spans
3. This shows up as a timeline in the Workload view

## Best practices

- **Link every activity to an output** — if an activity doesn't contribute to a result, question whether it's needed
- **Keep activities specific** — "Conduct 5 community workshops in District A" is better than "Do community engagement"
- **Set realistic milestones** — better to have fewer achievable milestones than many missed ones
- **Update status regularly** — weekly or fortnightly updates keep the team informed
- **Assign clear ownership** — every activity should have someone responsible
